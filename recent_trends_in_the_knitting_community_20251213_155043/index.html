1. The Post-Pandemic Landscape

The COVID-19 pandemic fundamentally reshaped the knitting community. Lion Brand, a 143-year-old yarn company, reported sales increases of 80% during lockdowns 4. Swiss retailer Galaxus saw craft supply sales surge by 646% from April 2020 to March 2021 4. The Guardian reported a 140% increase in people taking up crochet in 2020 11.

Evidence suggests the pandemic boom created lasting changes:

  • Etsy more than doubled its revenues in 2020 9
  • "We Are Knitters" saw 75% weekly sales increases during the crisis 10
  • NPD Group reported a 70% increase in arts and crafts sales in April 2020
Nuanced Picture

Not all retailers benefited equally‚Äîresults varied by location and product mix. Some industry vendors reported doing "only 10% of the business they are accustomed to" at virtual events.

4. 2024-2025 Style and Technique Trends

Multiple industry sources confirm the following trends 12:

Construction & Techniques

  • European shoulder construction: Shoulder seam shifted toward the back, popularized by Scandinavian designers like PetiteKnit
  • Texture emphasis: Cables, brioche, popcorn stitches, Fishermen's Rib, and bobbles
  • Chunky and oversized: Statement pieces including oversized scarves, sweaters, and cardigans
  • Marling: Pairing two contrasting colors for a heathered effect
  • Multi-craft combinations: Blending knitting/crochet with appliqu√©, embroidery, and beadwork

Colors

  • Pantone 2025 Color of the Year: Mocha Mousse (earthy brown)
  • Blue and white combinations for colorwork
  • Trending hues: sage green, terracotta, sun-washed lilac, butter yellow

Key Designers

PetiteKnit (Mette Wendelboe Okkels) has emerged as a dominant influence, with over 1 million Instagram followers 21. Her patterns including the Lyon Sweater, No Frills Sweater, and Sunday series have shaped Scandinavian-influenced trends.

5. Sustainability and the Slow Fashion Connection

The knitting community has increasingly aligned with sustainability values 121920:

  • Eco-friendly yarn demand: Rising interest in organic cotton, bamboo, hemp, and recycled fibers
  • Upcycling movement: Crafters repurposing thrifted garments and using reclaimed yarn
  • "Quiet luxury" aesthetic: Minimalist silhouettes with premium natural fibers
  • Slow fashion philosophy: Handmade as an opt-out from fast fashion's waste cycle

6. Mental Health and Wellness

Evidence suggests knitting and crochet provide mental health benefits, though claims should be viewed with appropriate nuance:

  • A University of Wollongong study of 8,000 crafters found 90% reported feeling calmer and 80% reported increased happiness 10
  • 70% of new knitters cite relaxation and stress relief as their primary motivation
  • A 2024 University of Gothenburg study found knitting brings "a sense of calm and gives life structure"
Important Caveat

A 2024 systematic review noted that while "there is some evidence that crafts-based interventions may benefit mental health and well-being," more high-quality research is needed. Critics note that crafting can also be "highly stressful and anxiety causing" for some people.

7. Community Controversies and Platform Dynamics

Ravelry Trump Ban (June 2019)

Ravelry, with approximately 9 million registered users and 1 million monthly active users 15, banned all pro-Trump patterns and content 2526. The ban made national headlines, sparked polarized reactions, and led to creation of alternative platforms including Freedom Knits and Fiberkind (18,000 members).

Ravelry Accessibility Crisis (June 2020-present)

Ravelry's website redesign caused reports of seizures (~8 users), migraines, eye strain, and screen reader failures 131415. Despite introducing alternative viewing modes, the accessibility issues remain unresolved as of late 2024.

Racial Reckoning (2019-ongoing)

A 2019 Instagram blog post sparked a multi-year examination of racism in the knitting community 1631. Responses included creation of BIPOC in Fiber directory 17, Vogue Knitting's Diversity Advisory Council 23, and the #blackpeopledoknit movement.

Knitting.com Debacle (2022)

Two e-commerce entrepreneurs purchased the knitting.com domain for $80,000 and described existing businesses as "grandmas with little blogs" 272829. The backlash was swift and severe, becoming a case study for online community platform revolts.

8. Local Yarn Shop (LYS) Ecosystem

Brick-and-mortar yarn stores face ongoing challenges:

  • In 2017, US yarn store count dropped below 1,000 (from 1,034 to 970)‚Äîlosing 64 stores in one year 35
  • Online competition and changing shopping habits threaten traditional retail

Community support efforts include Local Yarn Store Day (LYS Day), established by TNNA with its 7th annual event in April 2024 36.

What Is Established

  1. The pandemic transformed the community: Massive influx of new crafters, particularly younger demographics, fundamentally changed community composition
  2. TikTok drives cultural relevance: Social media has repositioned knitting and crochet as trendy, creative pursuits
  3. The market is growing: All sources agree the market is expanding, driven by DIY trends, sustainability, and mental health awareness
  4. Scandinavian design dominates: PetiteKnit and Danish designers have established aesthetic trends
  5. Community controversies reflect broader social tensions: Political polarization, racial equity, accessibility rights all manifest in the knitting community
  6. Sustainability matters: Eco-friendly materials and slow fashion philosophy are increasingly central to community values

What Remains Uncertain

  1. Exact market size and growth rates: Different research methodologies produce widely varying figures
  2. True demographic composition: Whether the community skews older or is more evenly distributed remains unclear
  3. Permanence of Gen Z adoption: Whether young crafters represent a lasting demographic shift is unknown
  4. Mental health benefit magnitude: While benefits exist, research quality is limited
  5. Platform future: Ravelry's dominance despite accessibility failures, and what alternatives may emerge, remains in flux
