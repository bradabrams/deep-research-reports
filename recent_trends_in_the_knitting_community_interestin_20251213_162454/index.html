Executive Summary

The knitting community in 2024 is experiencing a fascinating paradox: while long-term search interest has declined 48% since its 2013 peak, a vibrant revival is underway driven by Gen Z adoption on TikTok and high-profile celebrity endorsements like Tom Daley's viral Olympic knitting.

Knitting is my mindfulness, my meditation, my calm and my way to escape the stresses of everyday life.

— Tom Daley, Olympic diver & viral knitter

Style & Color Trends

HIGH CONFIDENCE

2024 Trending Colors

Multiple sources confirm that shades of blue have replaced hot pink (2023's dominant color) as the trending choice for knitters. Blue was prominent in Spring 2024 fashion shows and is frequently paired with white for colorwork designs.

Blue (2024 Star) Cherry Red Peach (Pantone 2024) Lavender

Garment Trends

Cardigans dominate garment trends. The cardigan has emerged as 2024's must-knit garment, appearing in multiple variations from delicate lacy options perfect for spring layering to the timeless librarian style. Longer lengths are particularly fashionable.

Nautical and preppy aesthetics are influencing knitwear design, driven partly by Miu Miu's fashion influence. This manifests in chunky cables, bold stripes, and argyle patterns.

Technique Trends

HIGH CONFIDENCE

Fair Isle Colorwork

Fair Isle colorwork continues its multi-year dominance, with sources confirming it "has become the favorite colorwork piece in the fashion industry this season." Large colorwork motifs are particularly popular for fall and winter designs.

Textured Knitting

Techniques like Aran cables and brioche stitches are in high demand, with knitters drawn to "thick, raised patterns which add depth and a rich look."

Additional Trending Techniques

  • Marling: Pairing two contrasting yarns for a heathered effect
  • Fuzzy yarns with lace: Creating ethereal "halo effects" with mohair or suri alpaca
  • European Shoulder Construction: A Scandinavian-influenced technique shifting shoulder seams toward the back

Tom Daley's Olympic Knitting

The British diver went viral at the Paris 2024 Olympics, spotted knitting a red, white, and blue "Paris 24" sweater featuring French and British flags between diving events. His visibility brought mainstream attention to the craft.

Note: Daley's TV show "Game of Wool" later faced criticism from Scottish knitters for misrepresenting Fair Isle techniques.

Community Dynamics & Tensions

HIGH CONFIDENCE

Ravelry Challenges

The platform experienced a 48% decline in engagement since its 2013 peak. A controversial 2020 redesign caused an accessibility crisis, with at least eight users reporting seizures and many more experiencing migraines, vertigo, and eyestrain. A complaint thread accumulated over 7,000 replies.

Political Divisions

Ravelry's 2019 decision to ban pro-Trump content created lasting divisions. Alternative platforms emerged including Fiberkind (18,000 members) and Freedom Knits.

More recently, post-October 7, 2023 tensions led Jewish crafters to create separate spaces like r/JewishCrafts (200+ members) where political discussion is explicitly off-limits.

Indie Dyer Challenges

LOW CONFIDENCE One report indicates indie dyers make approximately $2 profit per skein, making it "nearly impossible to sell enough skeins to make a livable wage."

Shopping Behavior

56% of yarn consumers report buying more online than in previous years, with pandemic-era shopping habits persisting. However, Local Yarn Store Day (April 27, 2024) continues to support brick-and-mortar shops.

Size Inclusivity

HIGH CONFIDENCE Size-inclusive patterns have become an industry expectation. The community consensus is that patterns should extend to at least 5X or 60" bust, with some designers offering sizes up to 70".

Areas of Uncertainty

Conflicting Data

  • Age Demographics: Survey data conflicts—one reports average age 58, while another shows even distribution across groups
  • Market Size: Valuations range from $4.7B to $7.2B for 2024—treat as estimates
  • "Dying Hobby" vs Revival: Both narratives have evidence—long-term decline is real, but recent reversal documented

Weak Evidence Areas

  • Male Knitter Statistics: "1/3 of young men" claim from single company survey without methodology
  • Indie Dyer Economics: $2/skein profit is anecdotal from single blogger
  • Mental Health Benefits: Dopamine release claim needs more scientific verification
  • Stitch 'n Bitch Revival: "Global resurgence" claims may be geographically specific
